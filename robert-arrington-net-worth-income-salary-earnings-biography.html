Robert Arrington Net Worth, Income, Salary, Earnings, Biography

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Wednesday, August 28, 2024

Robert L. Arrington (born October 19, 1938) is an American philosopher who specialises in moral philosophy, the philosophy of Ludwig Wittgenstein, and the philosophy of psychology. He received his Ph.D. from the University of California, Berkeley.

Arrington was born in Bainbridge, Georgia, and received his education at Vanderbilt University (B.A. 1960, with honours) and Tulane University (M.A. 1960, with honours) (M.A. 1962 and Ph.D. 1966).

In 1963, after three years as an assistant professor at The University of Southern Mississippi (1963–1966), Arrington accepted a position at Georgia State University, where he is now professor emeritus of philosophy and director of the Wittgenstein Institute. As a recipient of a Woodrow Wilson Fellowship (1960) and a National Defense Education and Research Agency (N.D.E.A.) Title IV Fellowship (1960), he also won the Griffin Award from the Southern Society for Philosophy and Psychology in 1968. He was a Research Fellow at the University of Oxford from 1974 to 1975, funded by the American Council of Learned Societies.
