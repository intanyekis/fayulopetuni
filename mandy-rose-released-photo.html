Mandy Rose Released Photo The Nude Picture Surfaced Online

Posted by Martina Birk on Friday, August 2, 2024

WWE star Mandy Rose’s career had altered in a matter of hours.

Following the revelation of explicit photographs from her FanTime website, a subscription-based content website akin to OnlyFans, Rose was let go by the wrestling behemoth WWE on Wednesday. 

She had dropped the NXT Women’s Championship the previous evening.

Less than 24 hours after abruptly losing the WWE NXT Women’s Championship to Roxanne Perez, Mandy Rose, a former NXT women’s champion, reportedly received a release from WWE.

At WWE NXT Deadline, where Perez won the Iron Survivor Challenge, she was slated to compete for the NXT Women’s Championship at New Year’s Evil on January 4, 2023. 

Rumors erupted on Twitter

Immediately after that match was abruptly shifted to the next night, rumors about Rose’s standing with WWE erupted on Twitter.

Rose’s release follows a number of nude image and video breaches from her FanTime page, a website that charges for content similar to OnlyFans.

‘Due to the material she was publishing on her FanTime page, WWE officials felt they were put in a difficult situation. They believed it to be outside the bounds of her WWE contract, according to Fightful Select.’

The extreme erotic video created by Rose was outside the bounds of her WWE contract, according to a report from the wrestling news website Fightful Select.

Pictures from Rose’s $40 monthly FanTime page over the weekend were published on social media. 

Pictures of Rose in a bikini and passionate shower scenes with her fiancé, the former WWE wrestler Tino Sabbatelli, are also included.

After becoming the first runner-up in Tough Enough’s reality competition, Rose’s WWE career got underway in 2015. 

She then joined the cast of the E! series Total Divas cast and eventually started a tag team with her real-life best friend, Sonya Deville.

Mandy Rose most recently competed in professional wrestling in NXT, WWE’s developmental division.

She held the women’s championship for 413 days before dropping it to Roxanne Perez on Tuesday night.

In contrast to what Rose created on FanTime, the WWE hasn’t marketed Rose’s character as being too s*xualized in the past during its lauded Attitude Era in the late ’90s. 

The WWE has previously imposed restrictions on its wrestlers’ extracurricular activities.

According to the sources, former head honcho Vince McMahon threatened to fire wrestlers who used services like Twitch and Cameo without getting permission from the organization.

Zelina Vega, another wrestler, was let go by WWE in November 2020 when she created an OnlyFans account. The year after she deleted the OnlyFans page, Vega was rehired.
