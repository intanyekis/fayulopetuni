Darkest Hour (2017) - $150 Million

Darkest Hour (2017) stands out in Gary Oldman's career, despite its comparatively modest box office earnings of $150 Million. Oldman received much praise for his depiction of Winston Churchill and won the Best Actor Academy Award. The film's focus on Churchill's pivotal role during World War II resonated with audiences, showcasing Oldman's ability to bring historical figures to life. While not the highest-grossing on the list, Darkest Hour remains a testament to Oldman's exceptional acting prowess and impact on critical and cinematic success.

Kung Fu Panda 2 (2011) - $665 Million

Gary Oldman provided the voice of the evil peacock Lord Shen in this hilarious movie. The adorable panda Po and his companions' experiences as they dealt with a fresh peril were continued in the animated sequel. Oldman's charismatic performance and the film's vibrant animation contributed to an astounding $$665 million worldwide.

Dawn of the Planet (2014) -$710 Million

This science fiction film Dawn of the Planet of the Apes (2014), grossed an impressive $ 710 Million worldwide. Gary Oldman's riveting performance contributed to the success of this franchise instalment, in which humans and intelligent primates battled for supremacy. The film's nuanced narrative, breathtaking illustrations, and Oldman's contribution cemented its position as a single of his highest-grossing projects.

Harry Potter( the Prisoner of Azkaban) - $796.7 Million

The global box office take for Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban, the last film in the series, was an astounding $796.7 million. Adding complexity to the wizarding world, Gary Oldman's portrayal of Sirius Black captivated moviegoers and helped the film's performance at the box office. The combination of Oldman's charismatic performance and the thrilling plot made this film an instant classic.

Harry Potter (Order of the Phoenix) (2007) - $942 Million

Gary Oldman returned to play Sirius Black in the 2007 adaptation of Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix. His performance garnered significant attention from viewers and played an essential role in the film's remarkable financial success, generating $942 Million in global box office revenue. The fifth iteration of the renowned Harry Potter series demonstrated Oldman's sustained appeal and the franchise's ongoing capacity to attract a dedicated following, consolidating its position as one of his highest-earning films.

The Dark Knight Rises (2012) -$1 Billion

The Dark Knight Rises, which came out in 2012, is one of Gary Oldman's highest-grossing movies. It made a whopping $1 Billion at the worldwide box office. Christopher Nolan directed the movie, the last part of his Batman trilogy. Gary Oldman plays Commissioner Gordon again. Oldman's captivating performance and the film's exciting plot and stunning visuals helped make it a huge financial hit and cemented his place as a blockbuster actor.

The Dark Knight (2008) -$1 Billion

The Dark Knight (2008), which starred Gary Oldman and was directed by Christopher Nolan, is one of his highest-grossing movies. It brought in a whopping $$1 Billion worldwide. Oldman's performance as Commissioner Gordon stood out in this classic Batman movie, which helped it do so well at the box office. The film was a cinematic masterpiece and a substantial financial success because of its gripping story, powerful performances, and original direction.

Transformers: Age of Extinction (2014) - $1.1 Billion

The 2014 film Transformers: Age of Extinction reached astounding heights at the box office, grossing an incredible $ 1.1 Billion worldwide. The presence of Gary Oldman in the movie increased its allure and demonstrated his aptitude for making blockbuster hits. The fourth film in the Transformers series captured moviegoers' attention with its action-packed scenes and larger-than-life grandeur, making it one of Oldman's highest-grossing productions.

Transformers (Dark of the Moon) - $1.1 Billion

Dark of the Moon was released in 2011 and is one of Gary Oldman's most successful films, generating a whopping $1.1 Billion worldwide. Oldman's appearance in the third chapter of the Transformers trilogy added to this film's allure, capturing moviegoers with its spectacular action sequences and amazing visual effects. The participation of Oldman in this blockbuster demonstrated his ability to contribute to box office success, as the film's significant earnings established its status as a prominent component in the franchise. The film's fantastic box office success was likely aided by its blend of Oldman's ability and the engaging subject.
