8 Of Taylor Swift's Most Iconic Looks

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Tuesday, May 21, 2024

As one of the most legendary singer-songwriters in history, Taylor Swift has influenced a generation through her music and fashion. She has worn some of the most iconic looks from her cardigan sweater, which inspired a song, to her marching band uniform from her "Fearless" World Tour. While the actress spent her early years favoring cowboy boots and simple dresses she has since developed her own unique style that millions now look to imitate. With one of the most timeless styles of any celebrity, her style somehow manages to be glamorous while looking effortless.

Related: How Taylor Swift's 2023 Tour Broke Records As Soon As It Was AnnouncedSome of the singer's most iconic outfits come from her tour looks, with her upcoming "Eras" Tour Swifties are going to see some truly spectacular looks and hopefully some callbacks from her previous 'eras' of style. Being in the spotlight for most of her life has ensured the public was able to see all of Taylor's best and worst style moments. With few bad fashion moments, the star cemented herself as a fashion icon for this generation with her timeless style. The songwriter's emphasis on staying true to herself and her own sense of style after years of people telling her how to dress is one of the reasons why her style has become so iconic.

10 Young Taylor Is Still Iconic

While her early years may not have some of her best fashion moments it certainly has some of her most iconic from the always-present guitar to the trademark blonde curls. The singer's love for delicate dresses and sparkles during this time made for some angelic looks that Swifties still love to this day. Taylor has always had a knack for being able to keep her ensembles simple yet gorgeous, it's clear to see how her style developed in her early years of fame.

9 Taylor Gets Bold With Red

After years of wearing subtly glamorous dresses, the director started rocking bolder colors and statement pieces getting more confident in her fashion choices. She famously started wearing the color red adopting it as a signature lip shade and even naming her fourth studio album after her favored color. During this time the producer embraced the vintage look getting inspiration from 50s-styled clothing and adding her own modern twist to make the look truly unique.

8 1989 Is A Fan-Favorite Fashion Era

With the release of each of her albums, Taylor's fashion changed to reflect the vibe of her style of music, for her first full pop album "1989" she started showing more skin and wearing bright colors. After rocking higher heels and more coordinated outfit sets her fashion made the full transition from country chic to city chic. This is a unique era in Taylor fashion as fans get to see the star wear a wide range of colors in different tones, unlike previous and upcoming eras where the star seems to restrict her color palette.

7 The Most Iconic Girls Night

Having one of the most iconic friendships in the entertainment industry, singers Taylor Swift and Selena Gomez regularly attend events together. They famously went to the 2016 Grammy Awards together where Swift went on to win three awards including making history as the first woman to win Album Of The Year twice. The star looked sensational for the event in a sultry neon two-piece set by Versace. She proved she was a matured fashionista ditching her ultra feminine dresses for more colorful, bold, sensual statement pieces that fans went crazy for.

6 From Country Pop Princess To Rebellious Superstar

As a star known for having a more colorful and delicate aesthetic, Taylor debuted a completely new look for her album "Reputation" taking inspiration from the dark and grisly HBO show "Game of Thrones". The director was going through a lot personally and reflected on this darker time in her life through her clothing and music. Her more sinister look was a shock to fans, but Swifties quickly got on board with the new aesthetic and supported the star through this time in her career. She has since gone back to adding more color and sparkle in her wardrobe but continues to include the dark edgy vibe from this time in her current style.

5 Back In Color

After the success of her sixth studio album, Swift had a highly publicized departure from her old record label Big Machine before signing to Universal Music Group in 2018. After signing her deal she released her seventh studio album "Lover" which became the best-selling album that year. The album reflected on a happier time in the singer's life after meeting her longtime partner British actor Joe Alwyn. With bright rainbows and calming pastels her clothing was meant to resemble the album's aesthetic. The rainbow tie-dye fringe outfit she wore to the iHeartRadio concert in 2019 designed by Jessica Jones has become one of her most famous outfits in recent years for its playfulness.

4 How To Do Cottagecore Chic

Taylor's seventh and eighth albums both released in 2020 were inspired by indie folk and alternative rock with a much more stripped down sound from Swift than fans are used to. Along with the toned down sound came the singer debuted a more down to earth style with cottagecore inspirations. The popular style was championed by Swift and some of her fellow celebrities and become a casual rural-chic alternative to their daily style. Her simple style during this time quickly became popularized by her fans who wanted to emulate the star's style.

2 A Floral Princess

In 2021 Swift attended the Grammy Awards in an embroidered Oscar de la Renta dress picking up the award for Album Of The Year for "Folklore". The floral midi dress is one of the artist's prettiest looks to date perfectly embodying her natural chic aesthetic. Her aesthetic gave her a mother nature vibe which including more sustainable clothing pieces with more emphasis on comfort and durability. The star has delighted fans with many style changes over the years each usually coordinated with a new album release, most of her looks have become iconic for their unique anesthetics.
